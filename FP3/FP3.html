The Supreme Court Data we are working with has 65535 instances with 61 attributes.
Some of the attributes are numberic and some catagorical.

Here are some attributes that would be useful for our initial visualization ideas:

Pie Chart - Issue Area, Date, Total number of cases(for a certain timeframe)(could be calculated within code)
Scatterplot - Decision Direction, Date
Line Graph - Date, Justice Name, Decision Direction? (potentially for a layer of interactivity)
Heat Map - Justice Name, Date

Some other potentially helpful attributes: caseId, caseName

Potentially a peice of our dataset could look like this:

caseId,dateDecision,caseName,issueArea,justiceName
1946-001,11/18/1946,HALLIBURTON OIL WELL CEMENTING CO. v. WALKER et al., DOING BUSINESS AS DEPTHOGRAPH CO.,8,HHBurton
1959-094,6/13/1960,PENNSYLVANIA RAILROAD CO. v. UNITED STATES,9,HLBlack
1965-133,6/20/1966,GEORGIA v. RACHEL et al.,9,JHarlan2

Case ID - a distinct identifier for each case.
Decision date - MM/DD/YYYY
Case name
issueArea - Numeric value representing a category for the issue
Name of justice voting in this instance
